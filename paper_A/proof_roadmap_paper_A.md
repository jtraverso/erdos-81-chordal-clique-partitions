# Proof Roadmap - Paper A

## Role in the series

Paper A proves the split-graph lower-bound and upper-bound benchmark for the Erdős #81 chordal program.

It is the independent entry point of the series:

```text
Paper A -> Paper D
```

Paper A does not depend on Papers B, C, or D.

## Main theorem

For split graphs on n vertices,

```text
max cp(G) = (1/6 + o(1)) n^2.
```

## Proof structure

1. Establish the extremal split construction.
2. Translate clique partitions into triangle-packing language.
3. Prove the asymptotic upper bound in the split setting.
4. Verify that the construction attains the constant 1/6.

## Use by later papers

Paper D cites Paper A for the sharpness/lower-bound side of the final chordal theorem.

No result in Paper A depends on the local chordal toolkit or on the fractional chordal theorem.
